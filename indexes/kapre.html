Kapre


Overview: The Kapre is a giant, tree-dwelling creature in Philippine mythology. Often depicted as a tall, dark-skinned being with a thick beard, the Kapre is known for smoking a large cigar and scaring people away from forests at night.

Physical Appearance

  • Large, dark-skinned humanoid figure, often depicted as being 7 to 10 feet tall
  • Thick, wild beard and hair
  • Usually seen sitting on a large tree, smoking a cigar or tobacco pipe
  • Has glowing red eyes and a deep, eerie laugh

Abilities

  • Can create an eerie smoke that confuses travelers
  • Capable of causing disorientation and fear in its victims
  • Can control the forest and trees, making them seem alive or dangerous

Weaknesses

  • Bright light or fire is said to repel the Kapre
  • Charms, such as those made from certain plants or roots, are believed to protect against it
  • Some believe it can be forced away by a powerful or courageous individual

Cultural Significance

The Kapre is a popular subject in Filipino ghost stories. It is often described as a prankster, sometimes playing tricks on people who wander too far into the forest. While it is mostly a frightening figure, some legends depict the Kapre as a protector of the woods, warning those who enter it at night.
